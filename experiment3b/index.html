Technical

Relation to inspring image:

  • Color: All colors were sampled from the inspiring image using the eyedropper tool from Aseprite available on Steam.
  • Primitives: The sun is rendered with `circle` primitives. The petals are rendered with `ellipse` primitives. The branches are rendered with `quad` primitives.
  • Polygons: The mountain is a single polygon generated with calls to `bezierVertex`. The tree trunk is also a single polygon generated with calls to `bezierVertex`.
  • Composition: Different random seed values lead to differently shaped mountains, sun positions, and differently positioned branches and petal clusters.
  • Life: The perspective of the image acts as if you were standing there and could slightly move right or left. The tree and its branches move slightly in the direction of your mouse cursor. The branches move as if they are affected by wind and the petals are also flowing as if wind was influencing them.

Reflection

While grinding through Experiment 2, I found myself becoming immersed in smaller and smaller details. For example, my branches are recursively generated which is definitely one of my best choices for my design. I think it adds a lot of spice when hitting the reimagine button. I'm hoping to continue working on the details of this project to make it even more imaginative and active. I really like the living portion of the impression and hope to add more.
